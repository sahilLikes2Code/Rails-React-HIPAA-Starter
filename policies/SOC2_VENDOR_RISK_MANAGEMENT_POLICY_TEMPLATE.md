# SOC 2 Vendor Risk Management Policy Template

> Tailor for your vendor inventory and document BAAs/DPAs where applicable.

## 1. Purpose
Establish a control framework for assessing, onboarding, and monitoring third parties in alignment with SOC 2 TSC CC1.3, CC9.2, and HIPAA BAA obligations.

## 2. Scope
All vendors that process, transmit, or store company or customer data, or that provide critical infrastructure services.

## 3. Policy Statements
- Vendors are categorized by criticality and data sensitivity before onboarding.
- High/medium risk vendors require security questionnaire + evidence review (SOC 2, ISO, penetration testing, etc.).
- Business Associate Agreements (HIPAA) or Data Processing Agreements (GDPR) are executed before data sharing.
- Vendors must support incident notification within 24 hours and cooperate with investigations.
- Vendor access is reviewed annually; offboarded vendors have credentials revoked immediately.

## 4. Procedure
1. Business owner submits vendor intake form (services, data types, integration diagram).
2. Security performs due diligence (questionnaire, certifications, pentest results).
3. Legal finalizes contractual controls (BAA/DPA, service terms).
4. IT configures least-privilege access and logging.
5. Annual reviews reassess risk, update documentation, and verify control adherence.

## 5. Monitoring & Evidence
- Central vendor inventory with risk tier and renewal dates.
- Records of questionnaires, certifications, and remediation plans.
- Alerts for upcoming contract renewals and missing BAAs/DPAs.

## 6. Roles
- **Business Owner:** justifies vendor need, ensures ongoing oversight.
- **Security/Compliance:** runs assessments, tracks evidence.
- **Legal:** manages contractual clauses and BAAs/DPAs.

## 7. References
- SOC 2 TSC CC1.3, CC9.2
- HIPAA 164.308(b) Business Associate Contracts
- GDPR Article 28 Processor Requirements
